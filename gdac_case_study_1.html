Case Study 1: How Does a Bike-Share Navigate Speedy Success?

Introduction

Cyclistic is a bike-share program that features more than 5,800 bicycles and 600 docking stations. To avail its program, customers can avail single-ride passes, full-day passes, and annual memberships. Customers who purchase single-ride or full-day passes are referred to as casual riders. Customers who purchase annual memberships are Cyclistic members.

Business Task

Cyclistic’s finance analysts have concluded that annual members are much more profitable than casual riders. In order to convert casual riders into members, the marketing analyst team aims to design marketing strategies. The team must understand the difference between casual and member riders by analyzing Cyclistic historical bike trip data to identify trends.

Summary

Casual Riders and Member Riders differ according to the following: 1. There are more Member Riders than Casual Riders. 2. Casual Riders spend more time riding than member riders. 3. Casual Riders ride more during weekends and afternoons. 4. Casual Riders use docked bikes. 5. Member Riders use classic bikes more often. 6. Member Riders use the bikes throughout the week and mostly from morning until afternoon. 7. Number of Riders peak during the months of June to October.

Recommendations

To increase casual rider to member rider conversion, the recommendations are the following: 1. Have a ride length limit for casual riders. 2. Promote benefits of biking during the morning and weekdays. 3. Have membership discounts during peak seasons.
